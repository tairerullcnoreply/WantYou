Community guidelines

We build WantYou together.

These expectations keep WantYou welcoming, respectful, and safe. Every feature—from anonymous introductions to reveals—works best when we share the responsibility for how we show up.

The guidelines apply to all activity on WantYou, including chats, lookup, posts, and events.

Remember to…

  • Ask for consent before revealing sensitive details.
  • Keep chats and posts free of harassment, hate, or threats.
  • Report behaviour that puts someone at risk.

Lead with respect

Treat every connection as a person first. No harassment, bullying, hate speech, or discriminatory language is allowed. This includes slurs, unwanted sexual content, or repeated advances after someone has declined.

Protect privacy

Never share private information—including screenshots of chats—without permission. Anonymous connections are designed to stay private until both sides agree to reveal.

Be authentic

Use accurate names, photos, and profile details. Impersonation, catfishing, spam, or deceptive activity undermines trust and will result in removal.

Keep each other safe

We expect everyone to set boundaries and respect the limits that others share.

What to avoid

  • Sexual content sent without consent or to minors.
  • Threats, intimidation, or encouragement of self-harm.
  • Sharing private or identifying information about someone else.
  • Automated or mass messaging that feels like spam.

What to do instead

  • Ask before escalating a conversation or moving platforms.
  • Use "Know you" or "Want you" status updates honestly.
  • Set your profile visibility and block tools when you need them.
  • Reach out to support if you feel unsafe or unsure how to respond.

Reporting & enforcement

Our team reviews every report with care and keeps reporters anonymous.

How to report

Use the “Report” option from any profile, message thread, or event. Include as much context as possible so we can understand what happened.

What happens next

We review for violations of these guidelines and our Terms of Service. Depending on severity, actions may include warnings, feature limits, or account removal.

Appeals

If you believe we made a mistake, reply to the enforcement email or message you receive. We will re-evaluate with a second reviewer.

Need more help?

For urgent safety issues contact local emergency services first. Our team can support with account questions, reporting follow-up, and privacy concerns.
